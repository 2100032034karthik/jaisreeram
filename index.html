Historical Significance

Lord Hanuman, revered as the epitome of devotion, bravery, and loyalty, holds a significant place in Hindu mythology. His unwavering dedication to Lord Rama and his heroic feats in the Ramayana make him a beloved figure across the country. The construction of the Anjaneya Statue aims to honor these virtues, inspiring devotees and visitors alike.

Architectural Marvel

Standing tall at approximately 135 feet (41 meters), the Anjaneya Statue dominates the skyline of Paritala village. The intricate detailing on the statue, from the muscular contours to the expressive facial features, showcases the craftsmanship of skilled artisans. The use of durable materials ensures that the statue withstands the test of time and natural elements.

Cultural Impact

Since its inauguration, the statue has become a major pilgrimage site and tourist attraction. Devotees from various parts of the country visit Paritala to seek blessings and witness the grandeur of the statue. The site often hosts religious ceremonies and festivals, adding to its spiritual ambiance.
